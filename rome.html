Why visit Rome?

Rome is a historic city. This means that wherever you go in the city, you'll see some amazing historical structures to marvel over. There are many Renaissance (and other centuries) wonders to visit, but there are some of those historic structures that aren't all that old. There are many stunning palazzi and piazze located around the city. The influence of the Popes is largely responsible for that. Over the course of many hundreds of years, they and their noble families commissioned countless public and private works to develop the city's distinctive architectural character.

Top-Rated Tourist Attractions in Rome

It's difficult to know where to start in a city that is so rich in monuments to Christian and ancient culture. Of course, your individual interests will determine your choices, but there are some locations that, like the Colosseum and the Pantheon, are virtually required landmarks of Italy and top tourist destinations worldwide. A word of advice: Be careful not to visit too many historic buildings or churches back-to-back when you explore Rome. And mix in a few less important but nonetheless iconic tourist destinations, like the Trevi Fountain and the Spanish Steps, where everyone goes to throw coins.
